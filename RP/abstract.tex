As urban cycling becomes increasingly prevalent, the need for advanced safety measures, particularly in mitigating risks posed by rear-approaching vehicles, is critical. Recent advancements in real-time vehicle detection and tracking present opportunities to improve cyclists' road-safety through the development of automated systems capable of alerting cyclists of potential dangers. This paper presents a vehicle detection and tracking framework that estimates both distance and orientation of rear-approaching vehicles from monocular image data. We introduce a simple method for vehicle angle estimation based on side bounding box characteristics and integrate this with a regression-based approach for relative distance estimation, utilizing depth map predictions. Additionally, we employ multi-object tracking to monitor vehicle trajectories and forecasting techniques to predict future vehicle positions and orientations using historical bounding box, angle, and distance data. These technical innovations could form the foundation of an automated road safety warning system, designed to issue real-time alerts to cyclists, thereby reducing the likelihood of collisions. 
\end{abstract}
